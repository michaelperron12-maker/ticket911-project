Quebec Law

Understanding Quebec Traffic Law

Quebec traffic law is governed by the Code de la sécurité routière (Highway Safety Code) — a provincial statute separate from the Criminal Code of Canada.

Code de la sécurité routière (CSR)

The CSR is Quebec's primary traffic law. It covers all moving violations including speeding, red lights, cellphone use, and careless driving. Violations are prosecuted in municipal courts across Quebec.

  • ✓  Governs all road users in Quebec
  • ✓  Administered by the SAAQ
  • ✓  Demerit points tracked on your license
  • ✓  Fines payable to the municipality

Quebec Demerit Points System

The SAAQ (Société de l'assurance automobile du Québec) manages the demerit points system. New drivers (learner/probationary) have a limit of 4 points. Regular license holders have 15 points before suspension.

  • ⚠  4 points — warning letter from SAAQ
  • ⚠  8 points — restricted conditions possible
  • ⚠  12 points — mandatory driver improvement
  • 🚫  15 points — license suspension (3 months)
