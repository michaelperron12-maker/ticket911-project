General Questions

What is Ticket911?
Ticket911 is the largest AI-powered traffic ticket defense platform in North America. Founded in 2011, we specialize in fighting traffic tickets across three jurisdictions: Quebec, Ontario, and New York. Our team of experienced lawyers — including former prosecutors — combined with proprietary artificial intelligence technology, has resolved over 105,000 cases with a 90% success rate. We handle everything from speeding tickets to DUI charges, and our platform allows you to upload your ticket, get an AI analysis, receive a quote, pay online, and track your case in real time.
How does Ticket911 work?
The process is simple and can be completed entirely online. First, you scan or upload a photo of your ticket. Our AI analyzes it in under 30 seconds, identifying the violation, searching relevant legal databases, and calculating your chances of winning. You then receive a personalized quote by email and SMS. Once you accept and pay securely online, our lawyers review the AI-generated defense, build your case, and represent you in court. You can track everything through your personal client dashboard. In most cases, you never need to appear in court yourself.
What areas do you serve?
We serve three jurisdictions. In Quebec, we cover all of the province including Montreal, Laval, Longueuil, Quebec City, Gatineau, Sherbrooke, and surrounding areas. In Ontario, we serve the entire province including Toronto, Scarborough, Whitby, Mississauga, Ottawa, Hamilton, Kitchener, and more. In New York, we serve all five NYC boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island. Our AI technology works across all three legal systems simultaneously.
Do I need to appear in court?
In the vast majority of cases, no. Our lawyers appear in court on your behalf so you do not need to take time off work or travel to the courthouse. In Quebec and Ontario, we handle the full court representation. In New York, we handle Traffic Violations Bureau (TVB) hearings and other traffic court appearances. In rare circumstances involving serious charges (such as DUI or criminal driving offenses), your presence may be required, but your lawyer will inform you well in advance and prepare you thoroughly.
AI Technology

AI Technology Questions

What is the AI Ticket Analysis?
Our AI Ticket Analysis is a proprietary artificial intelligence system that reads your traffic ticket (from a photo or scanned document), identifies the exact violation, determines the jurisdiction and applicable law, searches through seven legal databases (including CanLII, SOQUIJ, CourtListener, NYSCEF, and others), identifies relevant court precedents, and calculates your probability of successfully contesting the ticket — all in under 30 seconds. The AI reads tickets in French, English, and Spanish. It serves as the first step in building your defense, and its findings are then reviewed and refined by our human lawyers.
How accurate is the AI?
Our AI system has a 94% accuracy rate, which exceeds the performance of human lawyers in contract review tasks according to the LawGeex study. The AI is continuously trained on new court decisions, legal amendments, and case outcomes from our own 105,000+ case database. However, it is important to understand that the AI is a tool, not a replacement for legal judgment. Every AI analysis is reviewed by a licensed lawyer before any defense strategy is finalized. The combination of AI speed and human expertise is what delivers our 90% success rate.
Is my data safe with the AI?
Absolutely. We take data privacy extremely seriously. Our AI operates under a zero-retention policy, meaning your ticket data and personal information are not stored after the analysis is complete. All data transmission is protected by 256-bit SSL encryption, and our systems are PCI-DSS compliant. We do not share, sell, or use your data for any purpose other than analyzing your specific ticket. All information shared with our firm is also protected by solicitor-client privilege.
Can AI replace a real lawyer?
No, and it is not designed to. Our AI is a powerful analytical tool that accelerates the research and analysis phase of building your defense. It can search databases, identify patterns, and find precedents far faster than any human. But the strategic decisions — how to present your case, what arguments to make in court, how to cross-examine witnesses, and when to negotiate — require human judgment, courtroom experience, and legal expertise. That is why every case at Ticket911 is ultimately handled by a licensed lawyer. The AI makes our lawyers more effective, not less necessary.

Legal Process

Legal Process Questions

How long does the process take?
The timeline varies by jurisdiction and court scheduling. In Quebec, most cases are resolved within 3 to 12 months, depending on the court's calendar. In Ontario, cases typically take 4 to 12 months. In New York, TVB hearings can be scheduled within 2 to 6 months. The initial steps — ticket upload, AI analysis, quote, and payment — can all be completed within minutes. Once you retain us, we file the necessary documents immediately. You can track the progress of your case in real time through your client dashboard.
What happens if I lose?
If the court rules against you despite our best efforts, several things happen. First, our money-back guarantee kicks in if we were unable to improve the outcome of your ticket (you receive a refund minus the $100 admin fee). Second, depending on the case, we may recommend filing an appeal, which has its own separate process and fee structure. Third, your lawyer will explain exactly what the ruling means for you in terms of fines, demerit points, and insurance impacts, and advise you on next steps. You are never left in the dark.
Can I contest a red light camera ticket?
Yes, red light camera tickets can be contested. In fact, camera tickets often have strong defense possibilities. Common defenses include challenging the calibration of the camera, questioning the clarity of the photo evidence, disputing whether the light was functioning properly, and raising issues with the timing of the yellow-to-red interval. In Ontario, red light camera tickets do not carry demerit points but do carry fines, and contesting them can save you money. Our AI system specifically looks for camera-related defenses when analyzing your ticket.
What about insurance impacts?
This is one of the most important reasons to contest your ticket. A traffic conviction on your record can increase your insurance premiums by 15% to 40% or more, depending on the severity of the offense and your province or state. In Ontario, a speeding conviction can stay on your insurance record for 3 years. In Quebec, the SAAQ demerit point system can lead to license restrictions. By contesting and winning, you avoid the conviction entirely, which means no impact on your insurance. Even a reduction from a major to a minor offense can save you thousands in insurance costs over time.
How many demerit points before license suspension?
The threshold varies by jurisdiction and driver status. In Quebec, a fully licensed driver faces suspension at 15 demerit points within a 2-year period, but new drivers (with a probationary license) face suspension at just 4 points. In Ontario, the threshold is 15 points for fully licensed drivers, 9 points for G2 holders, and 4 points for G1 holders. In New York, accumulating 11 points within 18 months results in license suspension. Each type of offense carries a different number of demerit points — speeding can range from 1 to 6 points depending on how far over the limit you were. Our AI calculates the exact demerit point risk for your specific ticket.

Currently, our licensed lawyers practice in Quebec, Ontario, and New York. However, we can still provide useful guidance: our AI can analyze your ticket to help you understand the charges and your options, and we can refer you to a trusted partner lawyer in your province. If you are a Quebec or Ontario driver who received a ticket in the other province, we can definitely help, as convictions often transfer between provinces and affect your home-province demerit points and insurance.
