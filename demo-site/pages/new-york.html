New York Law

Understanding New York Traffic Law

New York traffic law is governed by the Vehicle & Traffic Law (VTL). How your ticket is handled depends entirely on where it was issued.

🏙 TVB Courts (NYC)

The Traffic Violations Bureau (TVB) handles tickets issued in all 5 NYC boroughs. The TVB operates under the NY DMV — not the court system. This means no plea bargaining is allowed.

  • 🚫  No plea bargains — guilty or not guilty only
  • ⚖  Administrative Law Judges (ALJs) preside
  • 📋  Officer must appear or case may be dismissed
  • 📌  Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, Staten Island

🏛 Non-TVB Courts (Rest of NY)

Outside NYC, tickets go to local town and village justice courts. These courts do allow plea bargaining, which often means reduced charges and fewer points.

  • ✓  Plea bargaining available — charges often reduced
  • ✓  Prosecutors handle the case (standard courts)
  • ✓  Speeding often reduced to non-moving violations
  • ✓  Long Island, Westchester, Upstate, all outside NYC

⚠ Driver Responsibility Assessment (DRA): If you accumulate 6+ points on your NY driving record within 18 months, the DMV charges an additional $300/year for 3 years ($900 total) on top of your fines. This is why fighting every ticket matters.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions — New York

Common questions about fighting traffic tickets in New York State.

What is the TVB and why does it matter?

The Traffic Violations Bureau (TVB) is a division of the NY DMV that handles traffic tickets in the 5 boroughs of NYC. Unlike regular courts, the TVB does not allow plea bargaining. You must plead guilty or not guilty and go to a hearing before an Administrative Law Judge. This makes experienced legal representation critical — your attorney needs specific TVB hearing strategies to win.

I'm a Canadian driver. Can you help with my NY ticket?

Absolutely. We specialize in helping Canadian drivers who receive tickets in New York. NY and Canadian provinces share point and conviction information. A NY conviction can affect your Canadian insurance and driving record. We handle everything so you don't need to return to New York for court or a TVB hearing.

What is the Driver Responsibility Assessment (DRA)?

The DRA is an additional surcharge imposed by the NY DMV if you accumulate 6 or more points within 18 months. The base fee is $300 per year for 3 years ($900 total). For each point above 6, you pay an additional $75 per year. This is on top of your fines and insurance increases — making it crucial to fight every ticket.

Do I need to appear in court personally?

No. In most cases, our attorney appears on your behalf. For TVB hearings in NYC, your attorney represents you at the hearing. For non-TVB courts outside NYC, our attorney handles all negotiations and court appearances. You typically do not need to be present at all.

How many points before my license is suspended?

If you accumulate 11 or more points within 18 months, the NY DMV will suspend your driver's license. At 6 points, the DRA surcharge kicks in ($300/year for 3 years). Points remain on your record for 18 months from the date of the violation. We fight to reduce or eliminate points to protect your license.

Can a speeding ticket be reduced outside NYC?

In non-TVB courts (outside NYC), yes — this is very common. Prosecutors frequently reduce speeding tickets to parking violations or other non-moving violations that carry zero points. In NYC TVB courts, this is not possible because plea bargaining is not allowed. Our strategy at the TVB focuses on fighting for a full dismissal or not-guilty finding at the hearing.
