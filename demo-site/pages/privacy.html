Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 7, 2026 — 9493-0971 Quebec Inc. (Ticket911.ca)

1. Information We Collect

When you use our services, we may collect the following personal information:

  • Full name, email address, phone number(s), and mailing address
  • Driver's license number and class
  • Traffic ticket information (photos, violation details, court dates)
  • Payment information (processed securely through our payment providers)
  • Communications between you and our team
  • Device information, IP address, and browsing data when using our website

2. How We Use Your Information

Your personal information is used to:

  • Provide legal services related to your traffic ticket
  • Process AI analysis of your ticket for defense preparation
  • Process payments and generate invoices
  • Communicate with you about your case (SMS, email, phone)
  • Improve our services and AI technology
  • Comply with legal obligations

3. AI Data Processing

🔒 AI Zero-Retention Policy

Our AI analysis engine operates under a strict zero-retention policy. Your ticket data is processed in real-time and is not stored, retained, or used to train AI models. The AI provider (Anthropic/Claude) does not retain any of your data after processing.

4. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures including:

  • AES-256 encryption for all data at rest
  • TLS 1.3 encryption for all data in transit
  • Dedicated servers in Canada — your data never leaves Canadian soil
  • Two-factor authentication for all staff access
  • Daily encrypted backups with 90-day retention
  • 24/7 intrusion detection and monitoring
  • PCI-DSS compliant payment processing

5. Solicitor-Client Privilege

All information you provide to Ticket911.ca in the context of legal representation is protected by solicitor-client privilege. This means your information cannot be disclosed without your consent, except as required by law.

6. Compliance

We comply with all applicable privacy legislation across our jurisdictions:

  • Quebec: Loi 25 (Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector) + LPRPDE
  • Canada: PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)
  • New York: NY SHIELD Act (Stop Hacks and Improve Electronic Data Security)

7. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services that may process your data:

  • Stripe — Payment processing (PCI-DSS Level 1 certified)
  • Twilio — SMS notifications
  • Google Analytics — Website analytics (anonymized)
  • Anthropic (Claude AI) — AI analysis (zero-retention policy)

8. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your personal information
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Withdraw consent for communications
  • File a complaint with the Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec
