🚨 Stunt Driving Alert

Ontario Stunt Driving — The Most Serious Traffic Charge

Since Ontario toughened its stunt driving laws, getting caught going 40+ km/h over the limit on roads with posted speeds under 80 km/h (or 50+ km/h over on highways) triggers automatic penalties that are among the harshest in North America.

🚫 Immediate Roadside Penalties

These penalties are applied at the roadside by the police officer — before you even see a judge:

  • 🚗  14-day vehicle impoundment — your car is towed immediately
  • 💳  30-day license suspension — effective immediately at roadside
  • 💰  Towing & storage fees — $500-$1,500+ out of pocket
  • 📈  Insurance increase — 100-300% premium increase for 3+ years

⚖ Court Penalties If Convicted

If convicted of stunt driving in Ontario court, the penalties escalate dramatically:

  • 💰  Fine: $2,000 - $10,000 — mandatory minimum $2,000
  • 🔒  Jail: up to 6 months — possible incarceration
  • 💳  License suspension: up to 2 years (first offence)
  • 💳  License suspension: up to 10 years (second offence)
  • 💳  Lifetime suspension (third offence)

Ontario Law

Understanding Ontario Traffic Law

Ontario traffic law is governed primarily by the Highway Traffic Act (HTA), with serious offences falling under the Criminal Code of Canada.

Highway Traffic Act (HTA)

The HTA is Ontario's primary traffic legislation. It covers all moving violations including speeding, red lights, cellphone use, careless driving, and stunt driving. Violations are prosecuted in Provincial Offences Act (POA) courts.

  • ✓  Governs all road users in Ontario
  • ✓  Administered by the MTO (Ministry of Transportation)
  • ✓  Demerit points tracked by the MTO
  • ✓  Stunt driving laws among harshest in Canada

Ontario Demerit Points System

The Ontario MTO tracks demerit points. Fully licensed drivers (G license) accumulate up to 15 points before suspension. Novice drivers (G1/G2) face suspension at 9 points.

  • ⚠  6 points — warning letter from MTO
  • ⚠  9 points — mandatory interview / novice suspension
  • 🚫  15 points — license suspension (30 days)
  • 🔒  Points stay on record for 2 years from offence date
